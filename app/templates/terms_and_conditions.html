Phase 1: Private Details and Information

  • All personal information shared by donors will be securely stored in our database using the latest encryption technologies to protect your privacy.
  • The public-facing information accessible on the platform includes only your first name, blood group, city of residence, and active status. This ensures that sensitive details remain confidential.
  • You are empowered to modify your personal information at any time by accessing your profile settings. This includes updates to your name, contact details, or blood group, ensuring that your records are always up to date.

Phase 2: Requirement of Being a Blood Donor

To be eligible as a blood donor, the following conditions must be met:

  • Age Requirement: You must be between 18 and 60 years of age. This is to ensure that your health is suitable for donation.
  • Health Status: Donors must be in good general health. This includes having a normal body temperature, no current infections, and being free from conditions like anemia, high blood pressure, or heart disease.
  • Weight: You must weigh at least 45 kg (99 lbs). This is to ensure that your body can safely tolerate the volume of blood donation.
  • Frequency of Donation: You can donate blood once every 56 days (8 weeks). Donors are encouraged to rest adequately between donations for the recovery of their health.
  • Exclusion Criteria: Donors with a history of HIV, hepatitis, or other communicable diseases are not eligible to donate. Additionally, individuals who have recently undergone surgery or have any active infections are temporarily excluded.
  • Consent: You must provide informed consent before donating blood. This includes understanding the possible side effects, such as dizziness or bruising, and acknowledging the medical precautions taken during the donation process.
  • Travel History: Individuals who have recently traveled to areas with high risks of infectious diseases may be asked to refrain from donating until they are deemed fit.
  • Pregnancy: Pregnant women and women who have recently given birth are not eligible to donate blood.
  • Medical Review: The donor will undergo a brief medical review, including a hemoglobin test, to ensure they are fit for donation.
